About Us

Keep Suffolk Green is a group of local Suffolk business professionals and academics abandoning long standing loyalties for the main parties to focus on what the green economy can do for our local area.

After the last six years, for many people, patience for the major parties has run out.

Not only have we had to endure a perpetual pantomime of political posturing, targets across the board have not been met up and down the country. Our Government has breached international law by not standing by our outgoing commitments to the EU, most notably regarding nearly zero energy building standards. They've opted for short term piece meal solutions while the rest of our neighbouring countries and forging ahead with new green economies.

We believe we can do better.

We at Keep Suffolk Green believe in a bottom up approach and we're starting on our own doorstep, our local wards here in Suffolk.

That's why on the lead up to this year's local elections on May 4th we're standing by our green candidates for a more prosperous, healthy more secure future.

Key Focus

Highly Energy Efficient Buildings

Suffolk is known for its beautiful, characterful towns. We get a wonderful sense of old Britain while walking the cobbled streets of our wonderful historic towns.

At times some of these old buildings will actually be incredibly energy efficient, however, more often than not they'll leaky huge amounts of energy. There are a host of solutions to this, both incredibly economic and sympathetic to the building's aesthetic.

Studies show that a highly energy efficient building stock positively impacts the local economy. It means that less money goes off to big energy companies and instead stays in home owners and businesses pockets, ready to spend locally on the high st.

Neighbouring counties such as Norfolk have the lead on us with this. They're an epicentre for very high energy efficient housing in the UK, with Norwich committed to all council buildings being built to the exemplar Passive House standard.

This is why, as local Green Party candidate I will make it a primary focus to ensure our river is cleaned up. For our local environment, economy and community.

Infrastructure

Leading on from upgrading our building standards, we also want to ensure that infrastructure is in place to cater for new developments.

We've all heard the stories of rivers up and down the country seeing high levels of pollution and this is in part due to infrastructure not being equipped to deal with higher levels of waste.

Our towns are borne from our rivers and inland estuaries.

They're a source of wealth still today, attracting visitors from all over the world to partake in walks and recreational activities on the water. They are our greatest asset.

The last thing our local economy needs is reports that they are being used a dumping ground for waste.

Smart solutions and green innovation can not only tackle the problems we face here locally, but can also inject new life into our local economy by creating new jobs and training opportunities for trades throughout the borough.

Local Economy

Ideas are great, however, you can't do anything without the money.

Empty shops in town centres were a sad familiarity before lock down and the situation has only become worse since.

Small to medium businesses struggle to stay afloat and compete against the giants of online commerce.

This guts the hearts of our towns, allowing money to flow straight out and into offshore bank accounts that pay nothing back into the local area.

Everthing we are pushing for is locally focused and driving to strenghten and secure our town economies.

We see a future of thriving local community led initiatives, creating opportunities in training, innovation and new jobs.

We must strive for a more productive, sustainable, healthy economic plan to insulate our towns from an ever uncertain future.
