Key Focus

The Health of Our River

Woodbridge was borne from the river.

Historically it brought wealth to the town and it still does today. People come from all over to visit our area of outstanding natural beauty and the Deben is central to this. It also provides physical and mental well-being for many throughout the summer and of course, was a lifeline during lockdown.

We've all heard about the stories of rivers being polluted up and down the country and sadly the Deben is no exception to this.

Local studies are showing high concentrations of E.coli as well as other pollutants and this is largely down to raw sewage overflowing from the sewage works in Martlesham.

Private water companies are under no legal obligation to do anything about this so it takes strong local leadership to hold them to account.

This is why, as local Green Party candidate I will make it a primary focus to ensure our river is cleaned up. For our local environment, economy and community.

Very Low Energy Housing

Our river problem is an extended planning problem. Another key focus for me is how we upgrade our planning requirements to drive low energy new builds and retrofits.

Low energy housing is cheaper and healthier to live in. It reduces our carbon output, creates a healthier indoor and local environment and means people are lifted out of fuel poverty which positively impacts our local economy.

Also new developments have to ensure that the adequate infrastructure is in place and the community development funds that are invested into the community must be invested in green, sustainable, local assets to further improve resident's quality of life.

Local Economy

High streets up and down the country were hit hard through covid.

Empty shops in a town centre were a sad familiarity even before lock down and the situation has only become worse since.

Protecting our river and upgrading planning will both feed into strengthening our local economy, but we can do so much more.
