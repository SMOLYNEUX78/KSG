  • Our Mission

    It's time for a change in how East Suffolk is run.

    We all want to feel happier, healthier, more fulfilled and more connected. It's no secret that what's good for our surroundings is also good for us.

    The Green Party plans to improve conditions for our families and communities. With smart, local solutions in our homes, our tansport and out workplaces. All of which will make life better, easier and cheaper.

  • Tom & Katie

    Steve’s a local dad and business owner whose connection to Woodbridge started over a decade ago, when taking a time out from London to visit a friend on the river.

    Such was his affinity with the area, he eventually moved to Woodbridge to start a family in 2015 and he hasn’t looked back.

    After running an arts charity for eleven years in London, Steve’s attention turned to how the art’s can impact environmental movements.

    He moved on to help start up the UK edition of market leading architect’s publication Passive House Plus and started to focus on a project seeking to incentivise low energy building in the UK.

    He’s been heavily involved in The Woodbridge Tide Mill Museum since 2018, recognising its iconic role in highlighting local green systems and celebrating the river.

    He’s an active member of the community, keen allotmenteer and composter, has been with a green energy provider since 2012 and has been a member of the Green Party since 2014.

  • Rachel Smith-Lyte

    An East Suffolk native, Rachel attended Farlingaye High school and has since worked for an environmental conservation charity in Norwich and at RSPB Minsmere, as well as in the Far East teaching street kids amongst other occupations.

    A long-time activist with Greenpeace and later Extinction Rebellion, she realised that with the looming climate and biodiversity crisis, she needed to get involved politically.

    She joined the Green Party in 2011 and has stood three times in local elections and in the 2015 and 2019 General Elections gaining 6% of the vote share thus retaining the Party’s deposit for the first time in the area.

    In May 2019 she secured her first seat - on East Suffolk Council – the first Green to be elected in the old Suffolk Coastal district. She is passionate about protecting the area’s natural environment from inappropriate development and the links between sustainable transport and improved air quality.

    Her other interests include localism and 'passiv haus' affordable housing as part of a post-growth society.
